Step 1 · ready to repost

The job post

Local SEO Specialist Needed for Ongoing Projects

We are looking for an experienced Local SEO specialist to help rank local businesses in both organic Google search results and Google Maps / local map packs.

The ideal candidate must have at least 3 years of proven experience working with local businesses and improving their online visibility.

Requirements

  • Minimum 3 years of experience in Local SEO
  • Proven experience ranking local businesses in organic search and local map packs
  • Strong experience with Google Business Profile optimization
  • Experience using SEO tools such as Surfer SEO, NeuronWriter, SEMrush, and/or Ahrefs
  • Experience working with AI tools such as ChatGPT and Claude
  • Proficient English writing and speaking skills
  • Ability to communicate clearly and provide regular updates

Nice to Have

  • Experience with AI automation tools such as n8n
  • Technical SEO experience
  • Experience creating or improving SEO workflows using AI tools

When applying, please include proof of previous results, such as screenshots from Google Search Console, Google Analytics, ranking improvements, traffic growth, or a short video walkthrough of your past work. Applications with clear proof of results will be prioritized.

We are looking for someone reliable, detail-oriented, and able to work independently on ongoing local SEO campaigns.

Compensation: $1,500 to $2,500 USD per month depending on experience, with flexibility for the right candidate. 30 hours per week, long-term engagement.

When applying please start your application with the word ODSEO in all caps.

Terms to state on offer

30 hours per week, $1,500 to $2,500 USD per month based on experience. Ongoing engagement on local SEO campaigns.

Band placement: $1,500 for a strong specialist who executes the checklist to spec. $2,000 to $2,500 for a senior operator with proven map-pack wins who can own client campaigns end to end with minimal oversight. Compensation is flexible based on experience; the range is a guide, not a cap.

Step 3 · owner: Daniel

Screening questions Proposed · adjust freely

Sent after shortlisting, before the Daniel interview. Built from the job requirements so answers confirm real fit before interview time is spent.

  1. Share one local business you moved into the map pack. Where did it start, where did it end up, over what timeframe, and what did you change? Attach proof (GSC, GA, or ranking screenshots).
  2. Walk us through how you optimize a Google Business Profile from scratch, and how you handle one that already has a messy or risky verification history.
  3. Which SEO tools do you use day to day (Surfer, NeuronWriter, SEMrush, Ahrefs), and what do you use each one for?
  4. Describe your content process with AI (ChatGPT / Claude). How do you keep the output from reading as AI-written and from failing AI detectors?
  5. How do you keep NAP consistent and clean up citations across directories?
  6. Have you built or used automations such as n8n in your SEO work? Give one concrete example. (Nice to have.)
  7. Confirm availability: 30 hours per week, timezone overlap with our team, and your monthly rate expectation within a $1,500 to $2,500 range.
Step 5 · the paid trial

Test task: OutriderIT service page

Given to the six shortlisted candidates. The task is to create a new service page for an imaginary business, OutriderIT, in NeuronWriter. These are recycled from old optimizations with the content deleted, so each candidate builds a fresh page. Each candidate gets their own NeuronWriter link (one link per client).

Must satisfy

  • NeuronWriter score 80+ (85+ preferred)
  • Readability grade 8 or 9 on hemingwayapp.com
  • 1,200 words (ignore NeuronWriter word-count recommendations for this test)
  • AI score 40 or lower on zerogpt.com
  • No em-dashes or other signs of AI writing
  • Nice formatting with a FAQs section
  • Optimized SEO title

Step 5 · owner: Jack

Trial scoring rubric Proposed · 10 points total

Step 7 · onboarding

30/60/90 day plan

The operating summary of the ramp. Dates assume the offer is accepted by Friday July 24 and the hire starts Monday July 27, which buys one overlap week with the outgoing SEO before he leaves July 31. If the start slips, shift every date by the same amount.

Days 1 to 30 · Learn, observe, integrateJul 27 to Aug 25
  1. Overlap week (Jul 27 to 31): structured handoff of all six client campaigns, SOP video review, full tool access confirmed (NeuronWriter, SEMrush, Ahrefs, GBP, GSC, GA), transition notes doc delivered to Jack by Friday July 31
  2. Daily async updates during the overlap week, then a weekly 1:1 with Jack
  3. By day 30: written status summary for all six clients, 3+ content briefs drafted, 3+ writer submissions QA'd to our standards, impact checklist applied to 2 campaigns, 2+ writer feedback loops completed
  4. Benchmark: zero missed priority deliverables caused by the transition
Days 31 to 60 · Own, execute, improveAug 26 to Sep 24
  1. Owns weekly delivery priorities across all six clients; Jack reviews samples, not every deliverable
  2. 6+ briefs completed with limited revision, 6+ writer submissions reviewed and improved, monthly reporting inputs prepared per client
  3. Impact checklist applied across all active campaigns; 3 practical AI or n8n automation ideas documented
  4. Benchmark: Jack spends measurably less time assigning and correcting routine SEO work
Days 61 to 90 · Contribute strategically, drive outcomesSep 25 to Oct 23
  1. Full delivery ownership with limited oversight; recommends next actions from ranking, traffic, and GBP data instead of waiting for tasks
  2. At least 1 automation improvement built or documented; short role playbook addendum written (briefs, QA, NeuronWriter standards, checklist, reporting, risk flags)
  3. Benchmark: Jack is no longer the default owner of SEO execution
Checkpoints and the day 90 gate

Formal reviews on Aug 25, Sep 24, and Oct 23 covering client continuity, throughput, quality, writer management, and how much delivery load has moved off Jack. Day 90 is a hard decision gate: keep (set the next 90-day goals), coach (written 30-day improvement plan with 3 to 5 measurable requirements), or cut.
